About SFD

Software Freedom Day (SFD) is an annual worldwide celebration of Free Software.
SFD is a public education effort with the aim of increasing awareness of Free Software
and its virtues, and encouraging its use.
Software Freedom Day was established in 2004 and was first observed on 28 August of that year.
Since that time it has grown in popularity and while organisers anticipated more than 1,000 teams
in 2010 the event has stalled at around 400+ locations over the past two years,
representing a 30% decrease over 2009.
SFD is being celebrated at National Institute of technology,Hamirpur.
Lots of events are being organised on that day along with few workshops.
Students will be introduced to many terms related to FOSS and Open Source.
